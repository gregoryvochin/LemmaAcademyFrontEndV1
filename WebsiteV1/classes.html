ALT-L - Coding, Math and Science - Level 1

A short course integrating computer programming with mathematics and science. Educational research has demonstrated that learning may be accelerated and deepened by assisting students to appreciate a few core concepts known as “keystone” concepts, which link to many other ideas within a particular discipline. This course aims to identify and develop such keystone concepts in mathematics and science using computer programming as a catalyst. Participants will be well prepared to excel in computer science, mathematics and physics courses at the secondary school level.

Cost: $500.00 (Please contact for details as schedules and durations vary)

Ages: 13-18

ALT-L - Coding, Math and Science - Level 2

Building on insights and learning techniques developed in ALT-L Level 1, participants explore early university topics in mathematics and science using coding and use graphics libraries to bring mathematics and science problems to life. In particular, Python code is used to combine calculus and physics concepts for deeper understanding, model forces, motion, kinematics and electronics, model the motion of the earth around the sun and model a galaxy.

Ages: 13-18

Robotic Design and Marketing

Work in an interdisciplinary team to solve robotics problems and document the process! Students develop interdisciplinary learning techniques to collectively solve challenging robotics problems, documenting the process and sharing it online. This experience offers strong development of leaderships and collaboration skills along with an extensive introduction to a wide variety of STEM-related topics including programmable electronics boards, basics of coding, operating systems, sensors and motors, CAD, design and production of 3D printed robot parts and robotics modules.
